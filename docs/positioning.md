# Greg Kowalczyk Consulting — Positioning & Angles

## Analysis

### The Transformation
Business owners go from: spending $75K+/year on agencies that deliver strategy decks and recommendations
To: owning custom AI tools and automation that are built, deployed, running, and that they control themselves.

From dependency → self-sufficiency.
From strategy documents → working systems.
From ongoing agency retainers → one-time builds they own.

### Competitive Landscape (Alternatives)

| Alternative | Weakness Greg Exploits |
|---|---|
| Do nothing (keep paying agencies) | $75K-125K/year bleeding out, still dependent |
| DIY (learn AI tools yourself) | Steep learning curve, years of trial and error, you're not an engineer |
| Hire a traditional AI consultant | Gets strategy docs, not working code. "Advise" not "build" |
| Hire a dev agency to build AI tools | $200K+ budgets, 6-month timelines, they don't understand marketing |
| Buy off-the-shelf SaaS | Generic, not custom to your business, monthly subscriptions forever |

### Unique Mechanism
**The Engineer-Operator-Builder Stack**: Greg isn't one thing. He's three things stacked:
1. **28-year engineer** (systems thinking, automation mindset, precision)
2. **Mid-seven-figure e-commerce operator** (tests everything on his own businesses first)
3. **AI tool builder** (actually codes, deploys, and ships working systems)

No traditional consultant has all three. Consultants advise. Developers code but don't understand business. Business owners understand the problem but can't build the solution. Greg does all three.

**Named mechanism**: "I build it, deploy it, and train you to use it."

### Market Sophistication
**Stage 2-3** in Ontario/GTA. "AI consulting" is growing but most practitioners are traditional consultants who added "AI" to their title. Very few actually build custom tools. The market has heard the promises but hasn't seen many people who actually ship working systems. Skepticism is rising. Mechanism-based angles will outperform pure promise angles.

## Angle Options

### Angle 1: The Anti-Consultant (RECOMMENDED)
- **The angle:** Greg builds working AI systems — he doesn't hand you a strategy deck and disappear. Builder, not consultant.
- **Why it works:** Every business owner has been burned by consultants who delivered documents, not results. The frustration is universal and visceral. By positioning AGAINST the category he's technically in, Greg becomes the exception they've been looking for.
- **Headline direction:** "I Don't Consult. I Build." / "You've Hired Enough Consultants. Hire a Builder."
- **When to use:** Default positioning. Works for all audiences. Strongest when prospects have been burned by previous consultants/agencies.
- **Psychology:** Enemy angle (the enemy is the consulting industry's advice-without-implementation model) + Contrarian angle (challenges "hiring a consultant" as the default solution).

### Angle 2: The $75K Replacement
- **The angle:** Replace $75K-125K in annual agency costs with custom AI tools you own — built by someone who did it for his own mid-seven-figure brands.
- **Why it works:** Anchors to a specific, painful dollar amount. The proof is Greg's own transformation. This isn't a promise — it's a documented result from his own businesses. The "you own it" element removes the subscription/dependency fear.
- **Headline direction:** "I Replaced $75K in Agency Costs With AI Tools I Built. I'll Build Yours Too." / "What Would You Do With an Extra $75K/Year?"
- **When to use:** When targeting cost-conscious business owners currently spending on agencies/freelancers. Strongest for e-commerce businesses with established marketing spend.
- **Psychology:** Specificity angle (exact dollar amounts) + Social proof angle (did it for himself first) + Risk reversal (you own what gets built).

### Angle 3: The Engineer's Advantage
- **The angle:** 28 years of engineering discipline applied to AI implementation — systems thinking that consultants and marketers simply don't have.
- **Why it works:** Engineers are trusted for precision, reliability, and systems thinking. In a market full of marketing-background "AI consultants," Greg's engineering pedigree is genuinely rare. Business owners trust engineers to build things that work.
- **Headline direction:** "28 Years of Engineering. Now I Build AI Systems That Replace Agency Work." / "Engineer-Built AI. Not Consultant-Advised AI."
- **When to use:** When targeting analytically-minded prospects (other engineers, operators, finance-types) who distrust marketing people. Strong for B2B and manufacturing/industrial businesses exploring AI.
- **Psychology:** Unique mechanism angle (the engineering background IS the mechanism) + Credibility through specificity (28 years, not "extensive experience").

### Angle 4: The Operator Who Builds
- **The angle:** Greg runs mid-seven-figure e-commerce brands AND builds AI tools — everything he recommends is tested on his own businesses first, with his own money.
- **Why it works:** "Skin in the game" is the ultimate trust signal. Greg isn't theorizing — he's using these tools daily on businesses that generate real revenue. Prospects trust operators over advisors because operators have consequences for being wrong.
- **Headline direction:** "I Test Everything on My Own Mid-Seven-Figure Brands. Then I Build It for You." / "Advice From Someone Who's Spending Their Own Money."
- **When to use:** When targeting fellow entrepreneurs and business owners who value practical experience over credentials. Strongest in e-commerce and DTC spaces.
- **Psychology:** Social proof angle (own businesses as proof) + Risk reduction (if it works for his businesses, it'll work for yours) + Identity angle (speaks to entrepreneurs who value doers over talkers).

### Angle 5: The Local AI Builder
- **The angle:** Burlington-based AI implementation specialist serving the GTA — not a faceless agency, but a local builder you can actually meet.
- **Why it works:** AI feels abstract and intimidating. A local, real person who builds things makes it tangible and trustworthy. Small/mid-size Ontario businesses want to work with someone they can shake hands with, not a remote agency.
- **Headline direction:** "Your AI Consultant in Burlington. Real Person. Real Builds. Real Results." / "AI Implementation for GTA Businesses — Built by a Local Engineer."
- **When to use:** For local SEO targeting. Google Business Profile. Local directory listings. When competing against Toronto agencies for GTA small business clients.
- **Psychology:** Proximity/trust (local > remote for service businesses) + Specificity angle (Burlington, Ontario — not "serving businesses globally") + Anti-corporate (person, not agency).

## Recommended Positioning Stack

**Supporting proof: The $75K Replacement (#2)**
The headline number that makes the anti-consultant claim concrete. Used in hero, results bar, and case studies.

**Credibility engine: The Engineer's Advantage (#3)**
Background credibility that answers "why should I trust this person?" Used in About section and bio.

**Trust signal: The Operator Who Builds (#4)**
"Tested on my own businesses" as ongoing proof. Used in case studies and how-I-work sections.

**SEO play: The Local AI Builder (#5)**
Woven into meta tags, contact section, schema markup for local search capture.

## Top 3 Pain Points (for copy downstream)

1. **Agency dependency drain:** Spending $75K-125K/year on agencies that deliver strategy documents, not working systems. Still dependent. Still not self-sufficient.
2. **AI overwhelm:** Know AI could help their business, but don't know where to start, who to trust, or how to implement without getting burned by hype.
3. **Theory-to-implementation gap:** Have read about AI, maybe even hired a consultant, but nothing actually got built and deployed. Still manual, still slow, still losing to competitors who figured it out.
